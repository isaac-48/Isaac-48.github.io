The Anatomy of an Electric Guitar Amplifier

The purpose of a guitar amplifier is exactly what the name implies; it amplifies and helps mold your sound as you play guitar. While some controls come and go, there are typically 6 main amplifier controls that you will find to shape your sound:
Gain The gain control allows you to determine the amount of signal from your guitar that is sent to the first amplification stage of the amplifer
Bass The bass control allows you to determine how much of the lower pitch frequencies of your guitar are present in the final sound leaving the amplifier
Middle The middle control allows you to determine how much of the mid-range pitch frequencies of your guitar are present in the final sound leaving the amplifier
Treble The treble control allows you to determine how much of the higher pitch frequencies of your guitar are present in the final sound leaving the amplifier
Presence The presence control behaves similarly to the treble control, except that it acts at a different amplification stage to shape the final sound
Master (Volume) The master control (also known as the volume control) determines how loud the sound leaving your amp will be
